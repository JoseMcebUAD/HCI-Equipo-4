# Personas

To capture the needs of the administrative staff at UADY Psychology Clinic I would select the following items of the toolkit:

**Existing practices:** Used to describe how they currently make tasks that the agenda module aims to support.

**Motivations and frustrations:** Used to describe what motivates them and what frustrates them about their current workflow.

**Attitudes:** Used to describe evaluations, feelings or tendencies toward ideas.

<mark>These specific items are relevant for the digital system because they define the requirements and architecture needed to support the administrative staff, it is necessary to understand the current manual process, identify frustrations translates to features, and understanding the staff approach toward technology determines the complexity of the UI.</mark>

To collect the items, the methods used are observation and interviews. First observing the secretaries while they work, because it is the most effective way to gather information about existing practices, after observing, conducting interviews with the staff covering the part of motivations and frustrations, and also attitudes. Once the data is collected, it must be processed with the analysis method and synthesis method, reviewing all the interviews transcripts and observation notes, writing down every important point, then having all the important points, putting them together into a single representation of the user type, and finally making a template integration.

Once the team understands these persona traits the design have an impact manifested in two ways:

**Impact on UI Design:** Design a visual calendar interface instead of a text-heavy list, to be able to immediately understand the clinic’s status without reading rows of data.

**Impact on System Logic:** The system logic will automatically trigger a form of message to the assigned psychologist when a secretary cancels or edits an appointment.
