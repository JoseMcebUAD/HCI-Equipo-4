# Process of Applying Personas Knowledge to UI

In short, the process of applying Persona knowledge to UI involves the following steps: first, define the goal; then, put the persona in a specific scenario; after that, use that scenario to map the interface; and finally, test and update.

<mark>Personas require scenarios; to get value from a persona, putting them into concrete situations is necessary. Scenarios dictate UI requirements; the story created for the persona answers the design questions, and the specific context leads to knowing what features are important for the user’s needs and how to achieve their goal.</mark> The design is iterative; a prototype should never be the final result. Testing the UI with real users and gathering actual data continuously helps in the process of updating the interface and persona designs to keep them close to reality.

### Application Example: ClinicaWeb Agenda Module

To apply Patricia’s persona to the ClinicaWeb Agenda Module, we translated her reliance on a physical notebook and her frustrations with WhatsApp messages into direct UI requirements. The inbox for new requests acts as Patricia’s single source of truth, by automatically queuing appointment requests and rescheduling directly next to the calendar, it eliminates her need to cross-reference WhatsApp messages and physical notebooks.

The new appointment modal integrates payment tracking by requiring a payment receipt upload, this captures financial data immediately, solving the frustration of manually checking if a patient has paid. Finally, the calendar mimics the simplicity of a paper agenda, by avoiding complex menus and text-heavy lists, it ensures a smooth, intuitive transition for a user who finds digital systems overwhelming.
