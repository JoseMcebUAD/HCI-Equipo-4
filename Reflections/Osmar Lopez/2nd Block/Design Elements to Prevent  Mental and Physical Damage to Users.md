# Qué elementos de diseño desde la perspectiva de HCI deberían ser tomados en cuenta para evitar que las herramientas que se diseñan eviten daños físicos o mentales a los propios usuarios?


With the recent uprising of social media platforms with short form content like Tiktok or Instagram Reels, questions about the consequences of overexposure to what is often referred to as “brainrot" content, it's going to do to the future generations. Some recent studies have shown that these type of content can lead to stress, mental fatigue and in extreme cases form an obsessive type of addiction known as "doomscroll"[1].

To better understand these emotional reactions and to design tools that help prevent them, we can incorporate aspects of affective design into our products.

Affective design refers to creating elements that evoke an emotional response, these can be as simple as to how are your users reacting to which colors are you using. Every single one of your components will evoke a reaction out of your users

<mark>From an ethical HCI perspective, designers must evaluate the intention behind these affective elements. Are they being used to support the user’s goals and well-being, or to maximize screen time and platform dependency?. Designing for well-being may involve intentionally implementing features such as usage reminders, content limits, or built-in pauses to counteract compulsive behaviors</mark>
