# UI vs UX

Even though UI and UX normally go hand in hand in software development, there are key distinctions that engineers must understand in order to use each discipline appropriately and efficiently.

In Tom Ewer’s article “UX vs UI: What’s the Difference? (And How They Apply to WordPress)” [1], UX is described as “the cultivation of customer satisfaction and loyalty by optimally developing the usability and positive emotional response a visitor experiences with your site.”

UX designers have to identify factors that could make the users have a negative experience with your product and conduct diferent tests, such as usabilty testing, until these issues are adressed effectively.

By contrast, UI refers to the visual components that enables users to interact with the product that the through the UX processes. A UI designer is responsible for creating elements such as typography, icons and other visual assets that effectively communicate and implements the UX designer's vision.

To better illustrate these ideas in an example, consider the scheduling module.<mark> The UX designer focuses on how the administrative staff perceives and interacts with the module, identifying their needs, potential challenges, and overall experience. On the other hand, the UI designer creates the visual components that support their experience and ensures that the UX design is laid out accordingly</mark>
