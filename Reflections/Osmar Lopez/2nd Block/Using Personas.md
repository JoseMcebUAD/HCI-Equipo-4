# Using Personas

<mark>Personas are one of the most important elements of User Centered Design. They allow teams to focus on specific user types and align design decisions with real expectations and challenges rather than designing for a generic “user”. </mark>

It ensures us that our user interface design can be traced back to to user research by transofrming this user data into specific UI elements and that our prototype aligns with user's goals and expectations.

A specific example of how personas influenced the prototype can be seen in our scheduling module. One of the primary personas represents an administrative staff member who manages a high ammount of appointments and has limited time to navigate complex interfaces. Based on this persona, the design decisions prioritized clarity, speed, and ease of use.

This knowledge was implemented in the UI by incorporating features such as simplified scheduling flows, visible filtering options, and an organized inbox for managing scheduling and rescheduling requests.
