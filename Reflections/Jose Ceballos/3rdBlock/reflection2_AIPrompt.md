## AIprompt
As we know, AI tools have been reaching levels of thinking and skills that surpass many junior and mid-level engineers, even seniors, and one of the things that has been of great help, as a tool, is to help the team members to complete some of the tasks that were assigned.

For this test, the team has already written down metrics for a usability requirement. The "Visibility of main functions" was chosen for this comparison. As a group, we chose to do a manual task and classify it as a quantitative task, because we are measuring if the process of a main function (edit, cancel and create) is visible to the user, and we must have a 100% success rate, which took about an hour or so because we had to review it and finally agree on that. On the other hand, the AI (Gemini) gave us a 3-phase task that includes research, design validation and documentation, which took about 3 minutes.

In this case, the team believes that AI could not know the whole process, context and time that it takes to make these tasks, or some obstacles that the group has to overcome. Yes, it is a better well-rounded set of tasks, but it falls short of what the project is and does not provide much context. For instance, the AI tool said ("test every possible task, how many clicks the user does and if he found it helpful"), but we don't have that much time, and really, the stakeholders can't do every single task of the UI.

Overall, we think that we could use some of the things that the AI said, but also trying not to "over engineer" a process that could be that easy.
