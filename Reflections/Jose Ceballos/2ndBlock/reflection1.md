### What´s the difference between UX and IU? and how to apply it in real world
Nowadays, people tend to think that UI and UX mean basically the same thing. To fully understand and apply them to our designs, we need different approaches and techniques that allow us to do so effectively.According to the WPMU DEV Blog, <mark>UX (User Experience)</mark> is the experience visitors have with our sites rather than just the components and design. This means every interaction makes up a logical flow from one step to another—such as clicking a button, picking a product, and proceeding to checkout.

On the other hand, <mark>UI (User Interface)</mark> consists of the physical and technical elements that enable the user to interact with the digital product, such as controls and buttons, visually portraying the UX elements. A UI designer might decide whether to use a slider, a button, or an arrow to execute a specific task.

By these definitions, we can agree that UI is about aesthetics and visuals, <mark>while UX is about functionality and usability</mark>. They are different concepts, but both share the same goal: making our product professional and attractive.

These two concepts can be mastered by a single individual; in many organizations, a developer often acts as a UX/UI designer, and it is actually quite rare to have separate UI and UX designers in smaller teams.

Nowadays, with AI, we can build products much faster. However, AI often lacks the depth required by complex UX/UI demands and frequently overlooks Nielsen’s Heuristics. This is why we still need to study these principles to ensure our products remain visually professional and user-friendly.
