  As a very green engineer in this field, I had no idea what a persona was. I thought it was just imagining who would use the system and that was it. I was totally
unaware of the different categories, the impact of this technique, the why, the reasons—basically everything essential about it. 
  First of all, the classification into four perspectives is something that ensures you can’t use the same archetype for every project. Having to use your 
imagination, even being empathetic with the client, leads me to consider at least the following.
  Using this technique, beyond its other benefits, helps you avoid projecting your own desires and needs onto a program. Let’s take a moment to think about 
  Doña Norma, a 55‑year‑old woman, worn down by life, tired of the same routine, sick of paperwork. Doña Norma arrives at the Psychology faculty every day,
  sits in her chair (which squeaks), opens her notebook, takes out her water bottle with the floral pattern, and gets ready for the same thing as always. 
  A therapist calls her to change an appointment time, so she has to start checking availability. Doña Norma finds an open slot, just as a request comes in 
  for another appointment at that same time, so now she has to deal with another problem; she can’t even catch her breath, when she’s told she has to call a 
  certain patient to reschedule another appointment. Well, you get the idea.

  Personas aren’t just a process you go through to try to understand. It would be like telling a father not to worry about his child, when we ourselves don’t 
  have children. Doña Norma will feel all the stress from the notebook, the heat of the city, the irritating voices of her coworkers, the annoying noises from
  her surroundings, and when it’s time to work, this program—the agenda—will be the center of her work, the most important place she could have. It has to serve
  her, the kind of person she is, and not what we learned in school, or what we think looks nice, or what will look good on a CV.

  If this technique was created—whether it’s agreed upon or not, standardized or informal—it’s still a unique tool for connecting with the client, 
  with the program you’re building. For capturing everything a person will need so they can stop feeling the overwhelming urge to jump off a bridge.
