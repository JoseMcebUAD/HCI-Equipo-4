# UX vs UI

People often mix up UX and UI, and honestly, it’s easy to see why—they’re so closely connected that they almost feel like two sides of the same coin. But the difference is actually pretty straightforward once you think about it.
UI is the “look and feel” part: the buttons, sliders, colors, typography, and layouts. It’s the visual language that lets you interact with the app. If the UI is clean, intuitive, and responsive, you don’t have to stop and wonder how to click something or where to find it. It’s like the steering wheel and dashboard of a car—what you touch and see directly.
UX, on the other hand, is the bigger picture: how the whole journey feels. It’s about whether the app makes sense, whether tasks flow naturally, and whether you walk away satisfied instead of frustrated. UX is the driving experience itself—smooth roads, clear signs, and the sense that you’re getting where you want to go without unnecessary detours.
So while UI is about aesthetics and interaction, UX is about functionality and emotional response. You can have a gorgeous UI that fails as UX if it confuses people or makes them feel lost. And you can have a solid UX ruined by clunky UI if the visuals are messy or hard to use.
<mark> UI is how things look and respond, UX is how things feel and work. Both matter, and when they’re aligned, that’s when a product really shines.<mark>
