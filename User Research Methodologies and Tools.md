# User Research Methodologies & Application Guide  
*Based on the UADY Psychology Clinic Agenda Module case*

---

## 1. Methodologies Used

| Methodology | Status | Purpose |
|-------------|--------|---------|
| **Semi‑structured Interviews** | Conducted | Explore mental models, workflows, pain points |
| **Heuristic Evaluation** | Planned | Identify usability issues before user testing |
| **Usability Testing** | Planned | Observe real users performing key tasks |

---

## 2. How to Apply Each Methodology – Step by Step

### 2.1 Semi‑structured Interviews

**Goal:** Gather in‑depth qualitative insights about processes, roles, and perceptions.

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | **Define interview objectives** aligned with research goals (e.g., O1, O2, O3). |
| 2 | **Create a flexible interview guide** with open‑ended questions and predefined topics. |
| 3 | **Select representative participants** (e.g., secretaries, coordinators, therapists). |
| 4 | **Conduct interviews** in a comfortable setting (workplace or video call). |
| 5 | **Record and take notes** (with permission) to capture verbatim quotes. |
| 6 | **Analyze for patterns** – identify recurring themes and map to research objectives. |

---

### 2.2 Heuristic Evaluation

**Goal:** Detect violations of established usability principles (Nielsen’s 10 heuristics).

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | **Select evaluators** – at least 3, with usability experience, not involved in design. |
| 2 | **Prepare the 10 heuristics** (see list below). |
| 3 | **Define scope** – which parts of the prototype to evaluate (e.g., appointment creation, rescheduling). |
| 4 | **Perform individual evaluation** – document heuristic violated, severity (1–4), location, suggestion. |
| 5 | **Consolidate findings** into a matrix and prioritize by severity. |

**Nielsen’s 10 Heuristics:** 

1: Visibility of System Status
2: Match Between the System and the Real World
3: User Control and Freedom
4: Consistency and Standards
5: Error Prevention
6: Recognition Rather than Recall
7: Flexibility and Efficiency of Use
8: Aesthetic and Minimalist Design
9: Help Users Recognize, Diagnose, and Recover from Errors
10: Help and Documentation


---

### 2.3 Usability Testing

**Goal:** Observe real users performing specific tasks in the system.

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | **Define representative tasks** based on critical workflows (e.g., schedule during a phone call, reschedule, cancel, filter). |
| 2 | **Recruit representative participants** (e.g., secretaries with different experience levels). |
| 3 | **Prepare scenario and moderation** – use a functional prototype, moderator observes without interfering. |
| 4 | **Run sessions** – ask participants to think aloud, record screen and audio. |
| 5 | **Analyze results** – measure success rate, time per task, errors, and qualitative patterns. |
| 6 | **Translate into design requirements** – prioritize fixes based on observed issues. |



## 3. Tools Used or Suggested

| Tool | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| **Interview guide** | Document with open‑ended questions and topics |
| **Recorder / transcription tool** | Capture interviews for analysis |
| **Findings matrix** | Consolidate heuristic evaluation results |
| **Navigable prototype** (Figma, Sketch, etc.) | For evaluations and testing |
